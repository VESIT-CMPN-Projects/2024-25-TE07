Alphabet

The alphabet is a set of letters or symbols that represent the basic sounds of a language. In English, there are 26 letters in the alphabet, starting with A and ending with Z.

Examples of alphabet letters: A, B, C, D, E, F, G...

Example sentence: "All Boys Can Dance."





Phonics

Phonics is a way of teaching children how to read and write. It helps children hear, identify, and use different sounds that distinguish one word from another in the English language.

Phonological Awareness

Phonological awareness is the ability to identify and manipulate the sounds of language. When someone finds it hard to work with sounds and link them to letter symbols, this is often a phonological difficulty.

Phonemic Awareness

An important phonological skill is called phonemic awareness. It is the first step in learning to use phonics. When a child sounds out the letters of a word and blends them together, they are using phonemic awareness. A child without phonemic awareness will not be able to separate the sounds. They have to be trained to hear the individual sounds in words.





Spellings

Spelling was developed at the Bristol Dyslexia Centre by a team of specialists. The program has been used since 1999, with millions of children worldwide. It is designed for children 6-11 years old, although many older students find the program helpful.

Use Short Words

Prefer short, easy-to-read words. For example, use "big" instead of "large" and "happy" instead of "joyful."

Stick to Simple Sentences

Use simple sentence structures, such as "The cat is on the mat" rather than complex sentences with clauses.





Nouns

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. For example, the words "cat," "school," and "happiness" are all nouns. Nouns can be common, proper, singular, plural, or abstract.

Examples: dog, city, teacher, love





Adjectives

An adjective is a word that describes a noun or pronoun. It gives more information about a person, place, or thing. Adjectives can tell us what something looks like, feels like, or how it behaves.

Examples: happy, large, blue, tall

Example sentence: "The happy dog ran through the large park."
