Science for Class 3

Living and Non-Living Things

The world is made up of both living and non-living things. Living things grow, move, and need food, while non-living things do not. Let’s explore some key differences!

  1. Living Things: Living things can grow, breathe, and reproduce. Examples include animals, plants, and humans. 🌱🐶
  2. Non-Living Things: Non-living things do not grow, breathe, or need food. Examples include rocks, toys, and cars. 🚗🪨
  3. Food & Water: Living things need food and water to survive, while non-living things do not need anything to stay the same. 🍎💧


Our Environment

Our environment is everything around us, including air, water, plants, animals, and land. It is important to take care of our environment to keep it clean and healthy. Let’s learn more about it!

  1. Air: Clean air helps us breathe and keeps us healthy. We should avoid pollution to keep the air fresh. 🌬️
  2. Water: Water is needed for drinking, farming, and cleaning. We should save water and keep it clean. 💧
  3. Plants: Plants give us oxygen and food. Planting more trees helps make the Earth greener. 🌳


Human Body and Health

Our body is a powerful system that keeps us active and healthy. To stay strong, we need to take care of it by eating well, exercising, and practicing good hygiene. Let’s explore how we can keep our body healthy!

  1. Healthy Eating: Eating fruits, vegetables, and proteins gives us energy and helps our body grow strong. 🍎🥦
  2. Exercise: Running, jumping, and playing make our muscles and bones stronger. 🚴‍♂️🏃‍♀️
  3. Good Hygiene: Washing hands, brushing teeth, and taking baths keep us clean and protect us from germs. 🧼🦷
